The Taj Mahal

About the Taj Mahal

The Taj Mahal is one of the most iconic landmarks in the world, located in Agra, India. It was commissioned by Mughal Emperor Shah Jahan in 1632 in memory of his beloved wife Mumtaz Mahal, who died during childbirth. This magnificent structure symbolizes eternal love and devotion.

It is considered a jewel of Muslim art in India and a globally admired masterpiece. The entire complex includes a mosque, a guest house, and beautiful gardens that enhance its charm and beauty.

History

The Taj Mahal took around 22 years to complete, with an estimated workforce of 20,000 artisans, laborers, and engineers from across India and Central Asia. The main architect, Ustad Ahmad Lahauri, ensured every detail reflected the grandeur of Mughal craftsmanship.

The mausoleum is built using white Makrana marble, which was brought from Rajasthan. Precious and semi-precious stones like jade, turquoise, lapis lazuli, and onyx were intricately embedded into the walls, giving it a unique elegance. The symmetry of the Taj Mahal is a defining feature, and the structure appears identical from all four sides, making it an architectural wonder.

Tourism

Today, the Taj Mahal is one of the most visited tourist attractions in the world, drawing millions of visitors every year. It was designated as a UNESCO World Heritage Site in 1983 due to its immense cultural and historical significance. Visitors from across the globe come to witness its mesmerizing beauty, especially during sunrise and sunset when the monument changes color.

Tourists can explore the lush Mughal gardens, marvel at the intricate calligraphy on the walls, and enjoy guided tours that reveal fascinating stories about its construction and legacy. Special night viewing is allowed on full moon nights, adding to the magical experience of visiting this iconic monument.

To preserve its beauty, certain restrictions are in place, such as a ban on heavy vehicle traffic near the monument. The Taj Mahal stands as a testament to India's rich history and architectural brilliance, making it a must-visit destination for travelers.
