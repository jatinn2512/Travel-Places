Varanasi - The Spiritual Capital of India

About Varanasi

Varanasi, also known as **Kashi** or **Benares**, is one of the **oldest continuously inhabited cities in the world** and a sacred pilgrimage site for Hindus. It is believed that **Lord Shiva himself founded the city**, making it an eternal center of devotion and spirituality.

Situated on the **banks of the holy river Ganges**, Varanasi is renowned for its **ancient temples, vibrant ghats, and spiritual ambiance**. The city is **over 3,000 years old**, and its significance is mentioned in various Hindu scriptures like the **Rigveda, Mahabharata, and Ramayana**.

The **streets of Varanasi** are a blend of history and modern life, where age-old traditions coexist with bustling markets and scholarly institutions. The city is a major hub for **philosophy, yoga, classical music, and Sanskrit learning**.

Varanasi is famous for its **spiritual aura, age-old rituals, and grand festivals**. Every evening, the **Ganga Aarti at Dashashwamedh Ghat** draws thousands of devotees and tourists, creating a divine spectacle of fire, chants, and devotion.

The city's **cremation ghats, like Manikarnika Ghat**, are believed to offer **moksha (liberation from the cycle of rebirth)**. Devotees from across the world come here to perform the last rites of their loved ones.
